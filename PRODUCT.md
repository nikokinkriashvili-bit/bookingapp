# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

adaptive

## Users

Small Georgian service business owners and staff across multiple verticals (detailing shops, salons, clinics, trainers, and similar) — the broader vision this MVP is cut from, per the source BRD's role-based onboarding. The current pilot is narrower (Carbros' own detailing operation plus a small network of detailer partners), but the design should read as general-purpose enough to serve any of the 8 business types the platform supports, not detailing-specific.

Core job-to-be-done: get through daily operations fast — take a booking, check what's scheduled, update a job's status, confirm payment — between customers, often on a phone, without friction.

## Product Purpose

A booking/scheduling and basic invoicing platform for small Georgian service businesses. Owners onboard their business, set up services and hours, then run their day-to-day through job intake and a calendar view. It exists to replace ad-hoc WhatsApp/phone-based booking and manual (or absent) invoicing with a fast, structured tool. Success looks like an owner completing their daily flow (intake → schedule → status updates → payment) faster and with less mental overhead than their current process.

## Brand Personality

Warm and approachable, not corporate or clinical. Should feel like a helpful assistant a small business owner trusts, not enterprise software they're forced to tolerate. Still efficient underneath — warmth shouldn't come at the cost of speed.

## Anti-references

Cluttered, dense, form-heavy enterprise/accounting-style SaaS dashboards — the common failure mode for small-business tools that try to look "professional" and end up feeling like a chore to use.

## Design Principles

- **Speed over ceremony** — core flows (booking intake, status changes) should take seconds; every extra tap or field is a cost, not a neutral addition.
- **Warm, not corporate** — favor approachable, human tone over sterile enterprise conventions.
- **One shared design across platforms** — a single visual language across iOS, Android, and web; no per-OS reskinning to chase native conformity.
- **Legible at a glance** — an owner checking between customers needs information scannable at a glance, not buried in nested screens.
- **Multi-vertical flexibility** — the visual identity should feel equally at home for a detailing shop, a salon, or a personal trainer, not hard-coded to one industry's aesthetic.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA baseline: sufficient color contrast, adequately sized touch targets, readable type sizes. No additional specific accessibility requirements identified at this time.
